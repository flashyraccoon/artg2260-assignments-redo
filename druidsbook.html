About the game:

I created The Druid's Book as my final project of an artistic prerequisite course before applying to the Zurich University of the Arts.
Although only a prototype - the project spanned about 4 weeks, and it was the very first time I ever tackled such a task - the game features fully functional character control and animation, a menu, item-pickup, and player inventory.

Take on the role of the Druid, a seemingly ancient, yet surprisingly agile hermit with, and embark on a quest to brew the most powerful portions of all time!
The Druid's tome provides all the information you need to find and collect only the rarest ingredients. But beware - the magical forest is full of treacherous swamps and merciless pitfalls!

Tools I used:

  • Photoshop
  • After Effects
  • Unity
  • GarageBand
  • Audacity
  • pen & paper
  • text editor

The Druid's tome provides helpful clues as to where you might find the ingredients you seek.

Beware of the many dangers of the forest! - Traps in the making.

My work for the game:

While I worked on this project independently, I was able to consult a mentor on matters where I had no prior experience, such as coding and usin the Unity engine to create my very first game.
All artwork used in the game is original content created by me, with the exception of the backgrounds of the tome (player inventory) and the help page (controls). I utilized photoshop for the 2D assets, and After Effects to animate the player character. Furthermore I created a simple but atmospheric score with GarageBand and Audacity. The scripts for Unity came in part from the default Unity library, in part from a personal library provided by my mentor for use by all the participants. I also added some of my own code by piecing together individual lines of code from existing scripts, and changing certain parts to fit my specific functional needs. Lighting and other environment effects were created within Unity.

The forest holds many weird and wonderful plants that can serve you as ingredients.
