About the game:

Epic Taco Quest is a little game that came into being as a class assignment. The project called for teams of two to create games inspired by arcade classics from the era of the Atari 2600.

Mickey, the underground construction worker, is ready for a well-earned break, but, oh no! He accidentally left his lunch taco in the break-room!
It's now up to you to navigate Micky to the object of his desire. Dig your way back into the earth, around obstacles and dangerous creatures, ever closer to your delicious meal. But most importantly - do all this before the surrounding earth becomes too instable! Not only does Mickey's persistent digging weaken the soundness of the ground, but so do the munchies - soil-devouring creatures somewhat resembling Pacman, and other creatures that live underground

My work for the game

The work on this project was somewhat distinctly split into two areas. To start off, both my teampartner and I came up with pitches for games, and we ultimately decided on one general idea. We then together worked through the mechanics of the game, the general structure and artstyle.
My seperate individual tasks were to integrate all of the 2D assets that my teampartner created, as well as some free-to-use audio assets, into the game engine (GameMaker: Studio), and create animations from the supplied artwork. I further wrote all required scripts in the GameMaker-specific language, realized the mechanics from the drafting stage, and designed a simple AI for the NPCs so that they would move about randomly and around any placed obstacles.

The semi-transparent red tiles are only visible in the editor; they enable the "munchies" to navigate around obstacles such as solid rock.
